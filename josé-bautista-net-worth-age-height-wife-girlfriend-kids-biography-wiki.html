Early Life & Biography

José was born in a middle-class family in 1980 in the Dominican Republic. He came from an educated family where his mother was an accountant and financial planner, and his father held a master’s degree in the field of agricultural engineering.

José got educated in De La Salle High School. He studied business at Mother And Teacher Pontifical Catholic University.

José received offers to play baseball from various famous teams like the New York Yankees or the Arizona Diamondbacks in the initial times. But he turned down all of them. Ultimately seeing no other opportunity, he decided to record himself using a camcorder and send it to various colleges in America.

At this time, he got a chance to play baseball in the United States through an organisation. José got a chance to play at Chipola College in Florida from this place. The college was seeking an everyday player, and José was the best choice. He played in this college for two years until he was drafted.

José has a younger brother as well who followed the footsteps of José and was ultimately drafted to play at the Chicago Clubs.

Career

In 2000, José got drafted for the Pittsburgh Pirates at the 2000 Major League Baseball draft. Soon, he started playing for other teams like Williamsport Crosscutters, Hickory Crawdads, Lynchburg Hillcats etc.

The Baltimore Orioles drafted José in the Rule 5 draft of the 2003 season, and it was from here José made his Major League Baseball (MLB) debut.

Since José has played for so many teams within such a small period, it made him become the first player to appear on five different MLB rosters in one season.

Since 2007, José became the third baseman for the pirates. In the next year, José hit five home runs for the Pirates against interleague opponents like New York Yankees, Toronto Blue Jays, Chicago White Sox.

In the same year, the pirates exchanged José for another player, leading José to score three home runs and 10 RBI in 21 games for this team.

After hitting a .444 with 20 total bases, José was named AL player of the week. In 2011, he signed a 5-year contract for the Toronto Blue Jays. He played hard during this time and eventually achieved many prestigious awards.

Due to a hip bone injury, José retired early in 2013. He finished with a 259 batting average, 28 home runs and 73 RBIs.
